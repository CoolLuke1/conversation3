Bell towers were used for many things including calling people to come for church, counting the years someone had lived when there was a funeral, being struck every hour or quarter-hour, sounded if there was a fire, or warned of any incoming invasions. The original architect behind the tower was Bonanno Pisano. He was an Italian Sculptor that mixed Byzantine and Classical styles of sculpting. He stopped his work on the tower in 1298 since it was still leaning. 60 years later, Tommaso Pisano started his work on the tower and finished construction in 1172. In 1835, architect Alessandro Gheradardesca made the first restoration to the tower by replacing the subsoil with a large marble base. The tower is 55.86 meters tall and weighs 14,700 tons.
